# Getting Started with Data Journalism

I've been thinking a lot lately about the intersection of rigorous quantitative analysis and impactful storytelling. As a statistics student who reads the Financial Times daily, I'm fascinated by how data can tell compelling stories that drive real change.

## Why Data Journalism?

Data journalism combines two things I care deeply about:

1. **Rigorous analysis** - Using statistical methods to find truth in numbers
2. **Clear communication** - Making complex findings accessible and compelling

The best data journalism doesn't just present numbers - it reveals patterns, challenges assumptions, and prompts action.

## My Approach

Working at the ASUCD Innovation and Research Lab has taught me that good data analysis starts with the right questions. When I'm analyzing survey data or building visualizations, I always ask:

- What story does this data want to tell?
- Who needs to hear it?
- What action should it prompt?

## Tools of the Trade

My toolkit includes:

- **R and Python** for analysis and visualization
- **ggplot2 and plotly** for creating publication-ready graphics
- **Markdown** for reproducible reports

## What's Next

I'm currently working on several pieces examining AI industry dynamics, nutrition science controversies, and social media regulation. Each requires combining technical analysis with narrative clarity.

The goal isn't just to do good analysis - it's to make that analysis matter.
